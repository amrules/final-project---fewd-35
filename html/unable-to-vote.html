Where did you change your address?

During change of address transactions conducted in ADOT offices, the agency requires voters to opt in to have their voter registration address updated, rather than using an opt-out procedure as required by the NVRA.

This is a violation of your rights.

When Arizonans change their address online through ServiceArizona.com, the authorized website for ADOT’s Motor Vehicle Division (MVD), voter registration information is not automatically updated as required by the NVRA.

This is a violation of your rights.

Arizona has specific ID requirements that can be easy to misinterpret. The full list of the ID requirements can be found here. Fortunately, a provisional ballot should always be offered to voters unable to provide a valid form of identification.

If you attempted to vote at the correct polling place, and are registered to vote in the county in which you voted, your provisional ballot will be counted.

The ballot will be valid and counted after you provide ID to your polling location before 7:00 pm on Election Day OR at the county elections office within three business days.

You can view the status of your ballot to determine when your registration eligibility has been validated and your vote was counted by visiting the Arizona Voter View Application website.

All polling locations and vote centers have been chosen with special needs voters in mind.


The Arizona Center for Disability Law runs a hotline to address any election concerns for persons with disabilities. The ACDL also will file Help America Vote Act (HAVA) complaints. You can call: 602-274-6287 or 1-800-927-2260 from 7:00 AM – 7:00 PM on Election Day.
